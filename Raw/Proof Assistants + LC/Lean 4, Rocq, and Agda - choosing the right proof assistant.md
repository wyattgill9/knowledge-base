
**Lean 4, Rocq, and Agda occupy distinct niches despite sharing a foundation in dependent type theory.** Lean 4 has emerged as the fastest-growing system, combining strong automation with genuine programming capabilities and an AI-powered research ecosystem. Rocq (renamed from Coq in March 2025) remains the most battle-tested option with 35+ years of verified software projects, including CompCert—now qualified for safety-critical avionics. Agda offers the cleanest type-theoretic experience and uniquely supports cubical type theory with computational univalence, but trades automation and ecosystem size for foundational elegance. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize momentum and tooling (Lean 4), ecosystem maturity and industrial verification (Rocq), or type-theoretic purity and HoTT research (Agda).

## Three flavors of the Calculus of Constructions

All three systems build on dependent type theory, but their kernels differ in significant ways that shape everyday usage.

**Lean 4** implements a variant of the Calculus of Inductive Constructions with a countable hierarchy of **non-cumulative universes** (`Prop`, `Type 0`, `Type 1`, ...) and schematic universe polymorphism. Two distinctive kernel features set it apart: **proof irrelevance is built into `Prop`** (any two proofs of the same proposition are definitionally equal), and **quotient types are native** with their reduction rule handled directly by the kernel. Lean's consistency has been shown relative to ZFC plus countably many inaccessible cardinals, and the self-hosted type checker Lean4Lean can verify all of Lean and Mathlib.

**Rocq** is founded on the **predicative Calculus of (Co)Inductive Constructions (pCIC)**, the most feature-rich kernel of the three. It maintains a richer sort hierarchy—`SProp` (strict proof irrelevance), `Prop` (impredicative), `Set` (predicative since Coq 8.0), and an infinite `Type` hierarchy with **cumulativity** (types at lower levels automatically inhabit higher levels). Rocq 8.19 introduced **sort polymorphism**, allowing definitions to work uniformly across `Prop`, `SProp`, and `Type`. It also supports coinductive types natively.

**Agda** is based on **Martin-Löf intuitionistic type theory** with predicative universes `Set₀ : Set₁ : Set₂ : ...` and **no impredicative `Prop`** by default (an optional `--prop` flag adds one). The most fundamental difference is that **dependent pattern matching is primitive**—Agda does not elaborate patterns into eliminators or recursors, unlike both Lean and Rocq. This design enables cleaner syntax for indexed families but makes the metatheory harder to formalize. Agda also uniquely supports **induction-recursion** and **induction-induction**, powerful definition schemes unavailable in the other two systems. Its universe hierarchy is non-cumulative, requiring explicit lifting between levels.

| Feature | Lean 4 | Rocq | Agda |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core theory | CIC variant | pCIC | MLTT |
| Impredicative Prop | Yes (proof-irrelevant) | Yes (+ SProp) | Optional (`--prop`) |
| Cumulativity | No | Yes | No |
| Quotient types | Native (in kernel) | Via axiom or setoids | Via cubical mode or axiom |
| Coinductive types | No (encoded) | Native | Via copatterns/guardedness |
| Pattern matching | Elaborated to recursors | Elaborated to eliminators | Primitive |

## Tactic proofs versus programs-as-proofs

The three systems represent a spectrum from tactic-centric to term-centric proof styles, and this distinction profoundly affects the daily experience of working in each.

**Rocq is tactic-first.** Most Rocq proofs are written as sequences of tactics in Ltac (or increasingly Ltac2) that manipulate a proof goal step by step. The SSReflect dialect, developed for the Four Color Theorem and Feit-Thompson proofs, offers a more compact tactic style emphasizing small-scale reflection—encoding propositions as decidable boolean computations. Rocq also supports direct Gallina term-mode proofs and the `Equations` plugin for complex dependent pattern matching, but tactics dominate in practice. This multi-language approach (Ltac1, Ltac2, SSReflect, Gallina) provides flexibility at the cost of fragmentation—newcomers must learn which style to use when.

**Lean 4 is genuinely hybrid.** Tactic mode is entered via the `by` keyword, and term-mode proofs work seamlessly alongside tactics—you can nest `by` blocks inside term expressions and vice versa. The `calc` block syntax enables clean equational reasoning chains. Structured proofs using `have`, `show`, and `suffices` read almost like informal mathematics. This flexibility, combined with a single coherent language for both proofs and metaprogramming, means Lean users can choose the most natural style for each subgoal without friction.

**Agda is term-only.** There is no built-in tactic language. Proofs are programs, written by constructing terms through dependently typed pattern matching. The experience centers on **hole-driven development**: you write `?` placeholders, and the type checker tells you what type each hole needs. You progressively refine holes using editor commands—case-splitting (`C-c C-c`), auto-filling (`C-c C-a`), and refinement (`C-c C-r`). Equational reasoning combinators from the standard library (`_≡⟨_⟩_`, `_∎`) enable readable proof chains. This approach produces highly readable, self-documenting proofs, but requires more manual effort for goals that tactics would dispatch automatically.

## Automation ranges from industrial-strength to minimalist

The automation gap between these systems is one of the most consequential practical differences.

**Lean 4 offers the strongest and fastest-improving automation.** The `simp` simplifier rewrites using tagged lemmas. `omega` decides linear arithmetic over naturals and integers. `aesop` performs extensible best-first proof search. `norm_num` normalizes numerical expressions. The headline development of 2025–2026 is **`grind`**, a new SMT-style tactic combining congruence closure, E-matching, a Cutsat solver for linear integer arithmetic, Gröbner bases for nonlinear arithmetic, and case splitting with non-chronological backtracking. Beyond built-in tactics, Lean 4 leads in **AI-assisted proving**: Google DeepMind's AlphaProof achieved silver-medal IMO performance using Lean 4, DeepSeek-Prover-V2 is an open-source theorem prover targeting Lean, and Harmonic AI (funded at **$100M** in 2025) builds verified AI systems with Lean as the backbone. LeanDojo, Pantograph, and Lean-SMT provide ML and SMT integration.

**Rocq has mature, well-established automation.** `lia` decides Presburger arithmetic. `ring` and `field` handle algebraic identities. `auto`/`eauto` perform proof search using hint databases. **CoqHammer** combines machine learning with external ATP solvers (E, Vampire, Z3) for powerful automated reasoning. The `Equations` plugin handles complex recursion. Coq-Elpi embeds λProlog for sophisticated metaprogramming. Tactician provides ML-based tactic prediction. Where Lean's automation is newer and rapidly evolving, Rocq's is older and more proven in large-scale developments—but the gap is closing.

**Agda takes a minimalist approach.** The **Mimer** proof search tool (replacing Agsy since Agda 2.7.0) performs bounded-depth search with a 1-second default timeout. There is no equivalent of `simp`, `omega`, or `ring` built in. However, Agda's **reflection mechanism** provides a powerful escape hatch: the `TC` monad exposes type-checking internals, enabling users to build custom solvers (ring solvers and monoid solvers have been implemented this way). Instance search (similar to type classes) uses discrimination trees since 2.7.0. The practical reality is that Agda users write significantly more proof steps manually than users of the other two systems.

## Programming in a proof assistant: from practical to principled

**Lean 4 is the most viable as a general-purpose programming language.** It compiles to C code, uses **reference counting** (not tracing GC) for predictable performance, and exploits a "functional but in-place" optimization—pure functional code automatically performs destructive updates when reference counts permit. The system is **90% self-hosted**, demonstrating that substantial software can be written in Lean. It provides `do` notation, an `IO` monad, pattern matching, mutual recursion, and a growing standard library (Batteries). The C FFI supports calling external libraries. AWS uses Lean to formally verify their Cedar authorization policy language. The new code generator shipped in v4.22.0 (August 2025) lays groundwork for stack allocation and ownership-aware compilation.

**Rocq's programming story centers on extraction.** Certified programs written in Gallina can be extracted to **OCaml** (most mature), Haskell, or Scheme, with proofs erased during the process. CompCert, the verified C compiler, is the crown jewel of this approach—it has been **qualified for DO-178C avionics** as of March 2026. CertiCoq provides verified compilation from Gallina to C and WebAssembly. The extraction approach is proven for high-assurance software but adds friction: performance depends on the target language, `Obj.magic` casts may be inserted, and the extraction mechanism itself is part of the trusted computing base.

**Agda compiles via GHC** (the MAlonzo backend translates Agda to Haskell) or to JavaScript. The Haskell FFI is comprehensive, allowing direct interop with Haskell libraries. As a total language by default, all functions must terminate and all patterns must be covered. Performance is adequate but not optimized for production—the GHC backend inserts type coercions that add overhead. Agda 2.8.0 improved distribution significantly by shipping as a **self-contained single binary**, eliminating historically painful installation issues.

## Ecosystem depth reflects each system's heritage

**Lean 4's Mathlib** is the largest unified, community-maintained formal mathematics library: **~267,000 theorems and ~127,000 definitions** as of early 2026, with over 770 contributors and 1.9 million lines of code. Coverage spans algebra, analysis, topology, probability, combinatorics, number theory, and category theory. The Mathlib Initiative (launched October 2025) professionalized maintenance with a dedicated editorial team. Beyond Mathlib, the ecosystem includes Batteries (extended standard library), SciLean (scientific computing), and Reservoir (package registry). Lake serves as both build system and package manager.

**Rocq's ecosystem is broader and more diverse.** Mathematical Components (MathComp) provides deep algebraic structures built on SSReflect. **Iris**, the higher-order concurrent separation logic framework (2023 Alonzo Church Award, 2025 Most Influential POPL Paper), anchors a thriving program verification community. VST enables C program verification. CompCert demonstrates industrial-grade verified software. MetaRocq formalizes Rocq's own type theory within itself. ConCert handles certified smart contracts. The `rocq-of-rust` project (funded by Ethereum Foundation) translates Rust programs to Rocq for formal verification. Package distribution uses opam, and Rocq Platform bundles curated package sets.

**Agda's ecosystem is smaller but distinctive.** The **agda-stdlib** (v2.3) covers standard data types and algebra with an "intrinsic style" carrying correctness invariants. The **agda/cubical** library is the canonical resource for cubical type theory, covering univalence, higher inductive types, and synthetic homotopy theory. **1Lab** (1lab.dev) is a standout community project formalizing mathematics in Cubical Agda with beautifully rendered, browsable HTML output. agda-categories provides proof-relevant category theory. agda-unimath formalizes univalent mathematics using axiomatic HoTT. The ecosystem is research-focused, with fewer "practical" libraries than the other systems.

## Community trajectories are diverging

**Lean 4 has the strongest momentum.** It won the **2025 ACM SIGPLAN Programming Languages Software Award**. Five Fields Medalists have engaged with the system—most notably Terence Tao, who released a Lean companion to his Analysis I textbook and led the formalization of the Polynomial Freiman-Ruzsa conjecture in three weeks. Kevin Buzzard's EPSRC-funded project is formalizing Fermat's Last Theorem. The Lean Focused Research Organization (FRO), funded partly by Alex Gerko/XTX Markets and AWS, drives monthly releases. Industry adoption is growing through AWS (Cedar), Google DeepMind (AlphaProof), and Harmonic AI. Lean dominates the AI-for-math research space.

**Rocq retains institutional depth.** Backed by INRIA since 1984, it has an established Rocq Consortium for governance and funding. CompCert's March 2026 avionics qualification represents the highest level of industrial trust any proof assistant has achieved. The blockchain sector shows growing interest through rocq-of-rust and rocq-of-solidity projects. The rename from Coq to Rocq (motivated by the original name's problematic English-language connotations) completed with Rocq 9.0 in March 2025 and has been broadly accepted. AI integration is developing—Claude Opus 4.6 solved 10 of 12 Putnam 2025 problems in Rocq using MCP tools—but Lean remains the primary target for AI proof research.

**Agda serves a focused academic community.** Development is centered at Chalmers/Gothenburg University, with biannual Agda Implementors' Meetings (AIM XLII scheduled for April 2026). The community is small but dedicated, with ~2,800 GitHub stars. Agda is used extensively in type theory research and PL theory courses but has minimal industrial adoption. The 1Lab and agda-unimath projects attract HoTT researchers specifically.

## Tooling quality varies but is converging

**Lean 4's VS Code experience is best-in-class.** The InfoView panel shows proof states, types, and goal contexts interactively as the cursor moves—providing immediate visual feedback that fundamentally improves the proving experience. Unicode input via LaTeX-like sequences, semantic highlighting, go-to-definition, and incremental elaboration create a polished workflow. Lake handles builds and dependencies, elan manages toolchain versions, and doc-gen4 generates browsable documentation. A browser-based playground (live.lean-lang.org) enables trying Lean without installation.

**Rocq offers the most editor choices** but with more fragmentation. RocqIDE (GTK-based) ships with the distribution. Two competing VS Code implementations exist: VsRocq (official, LSP-based) and rocq-lsp (alternative, with incremental validation and WebAssembly support—Rocq 9.1 runs in browsers). Proof General provides the traditional Emacs experience. This diversity means users can find a comfortable environment, but no single option matches Lean's integrated polish. Package management through opam is mature, and Rocq Platform provides curated distributions.

**Agda's tooling is Emacs-centric but improving.** The agda2-mode for Emacs remains the most mature interface, with deeply integrated commands for hole-driven development. The VS Code extension (by banacorn) provides equivalent functionality with identical keybindings. Agda 2.8.0's move to a **self-contained single binary** was a major quality-of-life improvement, eliminating the historically complex installation process. The HTML backend generates browsable hyperlinked source code, used to great effect by 1Lab. Agda lacks a dedicated package manager—libraries are managed through git submodules or custom tooling.

## The newcomer's path through each system

**Lean 4** benefits from outstanding documentation: *Mathematics in Lean*, *Theorem Proving in Lean 4*, *Functional Programming in Lean*, *Mechanics of Proof*, and the *Metaprogramming in Lean 4* community book cover every angle. The Natural Number Game provides a gamified browser-based entry point. Lean Game Server hosts multiple interactive tutorials. The learning curve is steep (dependent type theory is inherently complex), but the unified language design—same syntax for proofs, programs, and metaprogramming—means there is only one language to learn. Active Zulip community provides responsive help.

**Rocq** has the richest educational literature. *Software Foundations* (7 volumes, all machine-checked) is widely considered the finest introduction to formal methods. *Certified Programming with Dependent Types* covers advanced proof engineering. *Coq'Art* provides comprehensive reference coverage. However, the **multiple proof languages** (Ltac1, Ltac2, SSReflect, Gallina) create genuine confusion for beginners who must determine which style to learn first. Error messages, particularly from Ltac1, are notoriously unhelpful. University courses worldwide teach Rocq, providing institutional support for learners.

**Agda** is approachable for functional programmers thanks to Haskell-like syntax. *Programming Language Foundations in Agda* (PLFA) by Wadler and Kokke is a well-regarded textbook. The interactive hole-driven workflow provides constant guidance from the type checker. However, the **heavy use of Unicode** (∀, →, ℕ, ≡, Σ) and the **lack of automation** mean that proofs require more manual effort. Universe level management without cumulativity adds verbosity. The smaller community means fewer resources for troubleshooting. Agdapad provides a browser-based environment for initial exploration.

## What Cubical Agda uniquely brings to the table

Cubical Agda deserves special attention as a capability neither Lean nor Rocq can match. Enabled via `--cubical`, it implements **CCHM cubical type theory** (Cohen-Coquand-Huber-Mörtberg), adding an interval type `I`, path types (equality as functions from the interval), transport, composition, and Glue types. The practical payoff: **univalence computes**. Where other systems treat univalence as an axiom (blocking computation), Cubical Agda gives it computational content—`ua : A ≃ B → A ≡ B` reduces during type checking. Higher inductive types (circles, tori, pushouts, truncations) are definable as ordinary datatypes with path constructors. Function extensionality and quotient types come for free, without axioms. The agda/cubical library and 1Lab project have built substantial mathematics on this foundation. Limitations remain: transport along indexed inductive types is experimental, and performance can degrade for complex higher-dimensional compositions. The `--erased-cubical` flag enables using cubical features for proofs while erasing them at runtime.

## Lean 4's metaprogramming deserves its reputation

Lean 4's metaprogramming framework is architecturally distinctive: **metaprograms are written in Lean 4 itself**, with full access to the compiler's internal data structures. The system operates in three stages: parsing (string → `Syntax`), elaboration (`Syntax` → `Expr`, with macro expansion as an intermediate loop), and evaluation. Users define new syntax via `syntax` declarations, transform it with **hygienic macros** (`macro` / `macro_rules` for `Syntax → Syntax` transformations), and write full elaborators (`elab` / `elab_rules` for `Syntax → Expr` with access to elaboration state). A monad hierarchy—`MetaM` for metavariables, `TermElabM` for term elaboration, `TacticM` for tactic execution, `CommandElabM` for commands—provides structured access to different levels of the system. The Qq library adds type-safe expression construction. This framework is how Lean's own tactics are implemented, meaning users have exactly the same power as the core developers. Neither Rocq's Ltac2 nor Agda's reflection mechanism offers this level of integration.

## Recent developments signal each system's direction

**Lean 4 (2024–2026)** has maintained monthly releases, reaching v4.29.0 by March 2026. The `grind` tactic, developed across v4.15–v4.23, represents the largest automation investment. The new code generator (v4.22.0) replaces the old compiler entirely. The Mathlib Initiative professionalized library maintenance. FRO Year 3 targets Std 1.0, async/await, and a performance dashboard. AI integration accelerated dramatically—Harmonic AI's $100M raise and multiple IMO/Putnam results demonstrated Lean's position as the primary target for AI theorem proving.

**Rocq 9.0** (March 2025) completed the rename from Coq, introducing a single `rocq` binary and splitting the standard library into Corelib and Stdlib. Sort polymorphism matured across 8.19–9.1, allowing quantification over sort qualities. CompCert 3.17 (February 2026) achieved DO-178C avionics qualification—an unprecedented milestone for verified compilation. The rocq-lsp project enabled running Rocq 9.1 in web browsers via WebAssembly. CertiCoq added WebAssembly as a compilation target.

**Agda 2.7.0** (August 2024) introduced Mimer as the new auto proof search tool and added discrimination trees for instance search. **Agda 2.8.0** shipped as a self-contained binary for the first time and removed the primitive cubical identity type in favor of user-definable alternatives. Agda 2.9.0 is in development with module checkpointing for performance improvements. The agda-stdlib published its first JOSS paper with v2.0.

## Conclusion

These three systems are converging in capabilities but diverging in communities. **Lean 4 is the clear choice for anyone prioritizing momentum, AI integration, and a unified language experience**—it is where mathematical formalization is moving fastest and where industry investment is concentrated. **Rocq is the right choice for industrial verification, extraction of certified code, and projects requiring a proven track record**—CompCert's avionics qualification is something no other proof assistant ecosystem can claim. **Agda is the natural home for type theory research, HoTT/cubical mathematics, and those who value the elegance of programs-as-proofs**—Cubical Agda offers computational univalence that neither competitor can match.

The most surprising development of 2024–2026 is not technical but social: AI systems are becoming proficient users of these tools, and this is reshaping which systems receive investment. Lean 4's dominance in AI-for-math research is creating a feedback loop of funding, tooling improvements, and library growth that may prove decisive for long-term adoption. For a newcomer choosing today, Lean 4 offers the best risk-adjusted bet—unless your specific needs align with Rocq's verified software pipeline or Agda's foundational research strengths.
